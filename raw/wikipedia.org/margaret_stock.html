Margaret D. Stock (born 1961) is an immigration attorney based in Anchorage, Alaska, and a retired Lieutenant Colonel in the United States Army Reserve. She is a recognized expert on immigration law as it applies to US military personnel and veterans. She is a member of the Federalist Society.[1]

Stock is a 2013 recipient of a MacArthur Fellowship.[2][3][4] She has taught law at the United States Military Academy in West Point, New York.[2]

She ran for the United States Senate as an Independent candidate in the 2016 Senate election in Alaska.[5]

Early life and education[edit]

Stock was born in Boston in 1961 and grew up in Wellesley, Massachusetts. She was the fourth of nine children. When she was six years old, her father had the first of several heart attacks, and he died when she was 15. The family was in crisis, and she spent time in a homeless shelter and a foster home. While in high school, she got involved the Civil Air Patrol but she dropped out of high school before graduating.[6]

A high school guidance counselor helped her gain admission to Boston University, where she enlisted in the Reserve Officer Training Corps. She completed paratrooper training at Fort Benning, Georgia, and later transferred to Harvard.[6]

Stock graduated from Harvard and Radcliffe Colleges in 1985 (A.B., with honors in Government); Harvard Law School in 1992[7] (J.D., with honors); Harvard Kennedy School of Government in 2001(M.P.A.); and U.S. Army War College in 2006 (Masters of Strategic Studies).[7]

Career[edit]

Stock led the development of three programs pertaining to immigration issues within the US military. The Military accessions vital to national interest (MAVNI) program, started in 2008, in cooperation with the Department of Defense and Department of Homeland Security, helped the U.S. armed forces to attract and retain foreign nationals with language, medical, and other skills critical to military readiness and national security.[2][8][4] She spearheaded the American Immigration Lawyers Association (AILA) MAP program, "which pairs volunteer attorneys across the United States" with military families in need of their services.[2][8][4] She also helped start the naturalization at Basic Combat Training program.[2][9]

Stock has also spent many years teaching. From 2001–2010 she worked as a professor at the United States Military Academy at West Point[2] first in the Department of Law, then in the Department of Social Sciences. She also taught at University of Alaska Anchorage[2] in the political science department for two years.

The Washington Times called her "the foremost authority on military veterans facing deportation."[10]

Stock has testified regularly before Congress on issues involving immigration and national security.[11]

Running as an independent, Stock came in third in the 2016 U.S. Senate election, winning 13.2% of the vote, approximately 5,000 more than Democratic candidate Ray Metcalfe.[12]
