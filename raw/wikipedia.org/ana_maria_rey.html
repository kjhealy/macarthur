Ana Maria Rey is a Colombian theoretical physicist, professor at University of Colorado at Boulder and a JILA fellow. Rey was the first hispanic woman to win the Blavatnik Awards for Young Scientist in 2019.[3]

Education[edit]

Rey earned a bachelor's degree in physics at Universidad de los Andes in Bogotá in 1999. On July 29, 2000, Rey got married. Two days later, she immigrated to the United States.[1] She got her Ph.D. in physics at University of Maryland in 2004.[4] She went on to work as a postdoctoral fellow at Harvard University.[5]

Research and career[edit]

Rey is a theoretical quantum physicist who works on ultra-cold atoms.

Awards and honours[edit]

  • 2013 by the MacArthur Foundation[4][1]
  • 2014 Maria Goeppert-Mayer Award of the American Physical Society.[6]
  • 2019 Blavatnik Awards for Young Scientists[3]
