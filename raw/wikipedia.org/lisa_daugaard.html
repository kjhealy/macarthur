Lisa Daugaard is an American criminal justice reform activist. She received a 2019 MacArthur Fellowship.

Biography[edit]

Daugaard was raised in Seattle, Washington.[1] She began attending the University of Washington when she was twelve years old.[2] She completed her BA in 1983.[3] Daugaard started graduate school at Cornell University at age 17, initially studying political science and earning an MA degree in 1987, but after repeatedly being arrested and disciplined for her involvement in the campus South Africa divestment movement, she left her PhD program to pursue a law degree at Yale University.[1][3] She graduated from Yale Law School with a JD degree in 1995.[3]

Daugaard received a 2019 MacArthur Fellowship for her criminal justice reform work.[4] The award citation noted her efforts developing Law Enforcement Assisted Diversion (LEAD), a program that diverts low-level offenders in King County, Washington from the criminal justice system into the social services system.[3]
